# Body Recomposition Research — Training, Nutrition & Grocery
*Prepared for: intermediate lifter (1–3 yrs), training for strength + cardio + calisthenics, goal = build muscle while losing fat ("recomp").*
*Method: multi-agent deep research — 22 sources fetched, 95 claims extracted, top 25 adversarially verified by 3-vote panels (22 confirmed, 3 refuted). Evidence grades below reflect those votes.*
*Date: July 2026*

---

## PART 1 — TRAINING (verified evidence)

### Volume: the main growth lever — HIGH confidence (3-0)
- **12–20 hard sets per muscle group per week** is the working target for trained men. Gains rise with volume along a diminishing-returns curve; no reliable advantage past ~20 sets for quads/biceps (triceps may benefit from more).
- Sources: Pelland et al. 2025 Bayesian meta-regression (67 studies, 2000+ participants); Baz-Valle et al. 2022 systematic review.

### Strength needs fewer sets than size — HIGH (3-0)
- Diminishing returns hit strength much sooner than hypertrophy. Practical: keep heavy strength lifts at lower set counts; spend extra volume on muscles you're growing.

### Split doesn't matter (much) — HIGH (3-0)
- At equal weekly volume, **frequency has negligible effect on hypertrophy** — pick 3–6 days/week by preference and recovery. (Frequency does help strength, so hitting lifts 2×/week serves the strength side.)

### Don't train to failure by default — HIGH (3-0)
- Failure is not superior to stopping short. Growth flattens past ~2 reps in reserve. **Program RPE 7–9 (1–3 RIR)** — same stimulus, less fatigue. Especially valuable in a hybrid program.
- Source: Refalo et al. 2022 meta-analysis (failure vs not: effect size 0.12, trivial).

### Cardio does NOT kill gains — HIGH (3-0)
- Concurrent training showed **no significant reduction in maximal strength or hypertrophy** vs lifting alone (Schumann et al. 2022; 43 studies, 1090 subjects).
- The one real cost: **explosive power** (SMD −0.28) — worse when cardio and lifting share a session. Protect jumps/sprints/olympic-style work by separating them from cardio.

### Cardio choices — mixed confidence
- **Cycling over running** to spare muscle: MEDIUM (2-1 vote — Type-I-fiber-specific, only 3 running studies).
- **Lift before cardio in a shared session**: HIGH (3-0) for lower-body strength (~6.9% better); order doesn't matter for hypertrophy.
- Scheduling flexibility: HIGH (3-0) — modality/frequency/same-vs-separate session don't significantly change the strength outcome, so fit cardio to your life.

### ❌ Refuted by the verification panel (do NOT act on these)
1. "Choose cardio dissimilar to your lifts; rucking is most tolerable" — 1-2, killed.
2. "Never do long cardio right after lifting" — 0-3, killed.
3. "Glycogen takes 72h to replenish after long cardio; plan volume around it" — 1-2, killed.

## PART 2 — NUTRITION (verified evidence)

### Protein targets — HIGH (3-0)
- Baseline for exercisers: **1.4–2.0 g/kg/day (~0.64–0.9 g/lb)**; hypertrophy plateau ≈ 1.6 g/kg (Morton et al. 2018).
- **In a caloric deficit (recomp): 2.3–3.1 g/kg/day** to preserve lean mass — practical anchor: **~1 g per lb bodyweight, edging higher while cutting**.
- Source: ISSN Position Stand (Jäger et al. 2017).

### Timing barely matters; totals rule — HIGH (3-0)
- Total daily protein is the strongest predictor of growth. The "anabolic window" adds nothing meaningful once totals are met — muscle stays protein-sensitized 24+ h post-training.
- Best practice: **20–40 g high-quality protein every 3–4 hours** (0.25–0.40 g/kg per dose).
- Sources: ISSN 2017; Aragon & Schoenfeld 2013 meta-analysis.

### App implications
- Track **daily protein vs. a per-bodyweight target** (headline metric) and an even-distribution sub-metric (# of 25g+ feedings).
- Do not build features around peri-workout timing windows.

## PART 3 — PRACTICAL TRANSLATION *(general programming knowledge — NOT verified by the evidence panel; clearly separated per the research caveats)*

### Example hybrid week (4-day upper/lower + cardio)
| Day | Session | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Lower (strength-bias) | Squat 4×4 @RPE 8, RDL 3×6, leg press 3×10, calf 3×12 |
| Tue | Upper (hypertrophy) + 15′ bike intervals after | Bench 4×8, row 4×10, incline DB 3×12, pulldown/pull-ups 3×AMRAP, curls/triceps 3×12 |
| Wed | Zone-2 cardio 30–45′ (bike) | Separate day = zero interference |
| Thu | Lower (hypertrophy) | Front squat/hack 4×8, hip thrust 3×10, leg curl 3×12, calf 3×15 |
| Fri | Upper (strength-bias + calisthenics) | OHP 4×5, weighted pull-up 4×5, dips 3×AMRAP, push-up variations, core |
| Sat/Sun | Optional: sport, walk, farmers market on foot | Recovery |

- Calisthenics counting: a hard set of pull-ups/dips/push-up progressions counts toward that muscle's weekly sets like any gym set.
- Progressive overload: when you hit the top of a rep range at RPE ≤8, add load ~2.5–5%; for calisthenics, progress leverage (e.g., harder variation) or add reps/weight.

### Calorie strategy for recomp (general practice)
- Start at **maintenance minus ~10–15%** (roughly 300–500 kcal deficit), protein per targets above, lift hard, and let scale + gym numbers arbitrate: strength climbing + waist shrinking = on track.
- Rate check: aim to lose ~0.25–0.5% bodyweight/week. Faster = eating into muscle; stalled 3+ weeks = trim ~100–200 kcal.

### Grocery strategy *(blog-quality sources only — flagged for deeper verification)*
- **Costco** = protein volume-buy base: chicken breast, ground beef, eggs, Greek yogurt/Chobani 20g drinks, frozen fruit, rice, oats.
- **Sprouts** generally beats Whole Foods on produce and bulk bins (basket tests: ~$245–285 vs ~$295–355), though Whole Foods won some staple baskets (eggs/dairy) and Prime discounts narrow the gap.
- **Farmers market** = produce quality/seasonal; not a protein value play.
- Suggested loop: Costco monthly (proteins/staples) → Sprouts weekly (produce) → farmers market when schedule allows.

### Late-night eating out *(blog macro data — verify menus locally)*
- Chipotle double-chicken bowl, no rice/beans: ~50g protein / ~450 kcal.
- Chick-fil-A Grilled Cool Wrap (no dressing): ~42g / ~350.
- In-N-Out Protein Style: strong protein-to-calorie if you skip fries.
- Rule of thumb: named grilled protein + vegetables, skip liquid calories.

### Family-flexible template meals
Protein (chicken / beef / fish / tofu) × Carb (rice / bread / tortilla / potato) × Veg (whatever's fresh) × Sauce. Cook protein + carb in bulk; family assembles their way, you portion yours to macros. Shopping list = family base quantities + your protein add-on.
